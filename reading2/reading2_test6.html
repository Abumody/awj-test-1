Visit to Bridgetown

Dear Jack,

Thanks for your letter. I'm really excited to hear that you and your family are planning to visit my home town while you're in England this summer and of course, I would be happy to give you the advice you asked for.

The best place to stay in is probably The King's Arms Hotel in the town center, quite close to the railway station. However, if you prefer to stay by the sea, The Ship Inn Hotel is very comfortable and the prices are reasonable, too. It costs around 60 pounds for double room per night. Let me know if you would like me to reserve any rooms for you.

If I were you, I would certainly go to the beach. Although it isn't a good idea to swim in the sea (it's too cold!), you could have a look around the old harbour and do some fishing or go in a boat trip. Also, you can visit the funfair or have a ride on a horse. Besides that, yearly every summer we have a local festival with outdoor activities for kids, local food and some traditional sports competitions. Furthermore, down the coast is Bridgetown Castle, which is worth visiting. And just outside the castle there are many souvenir shops. There isn't much nightlife, though, I'm afraid! Bridgetown is a quiet place. However, we do have a few good restaurants. My favourite is The Red Herring, which serves delicious fresh fish. And for your vegetarian wife there are many restaurants that offer full vegetarian menus.

It would be a good idea if you go shopping on your last day. There are no big shopping malls here, but we have an outlet in which you will find everything you need.

I'm sure you will enjoy a short visit to Bridgetown, and I hope we will be able to meet up so I can show you around myself. I can't wait!

Lots of love,
Steve
