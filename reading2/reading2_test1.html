Applying for a Job

Dear Sir/Madam,

I would like to apply for the job of a tour guide for your Overland Africa tours. I have had lots of useful experience for this job.

I have spent five months backpacking around Africa. In 2018, I visited Ethiopia, Kenya and Tanzania. This trip taught me to be independent and to organise travel arrangements myself such as transports, accommodation, food and visas. I enjoyed meeting local people, learning about local traditions and visiting natural parks. I read and learned a lot about the culture and wildlife, and I even learned some Swahili and Afrikaans.

I have also worked as a leader at a children's summer camp in the south of England. I worked there in 2017. I led activities for children aged 9–12, including football, climbing and campfire songs and games. For this work, I received training in climbing instructions and First aid. During this time, I developed many useful skills. For example, I had to ensure the children were always safe and happy. I learned how to be helpful and positive, and I learned ways to entertain people and help them to be more social, make friends when they are with strangers.

In addition, I have completed the Active Youth Challenge. I did this while I was at high school in 2016. To complete this challenge, I firstly had to volunteer with a local organisation. I helped at a social club for the elderly, where I chatted and played games with the members. This experience taught me to be patient and friendly. Secondly, I had to learn something by myself. I learned car mechanics, which taught me some useful, practical skills. Thirdly, I organised a four-day camping and hiking trip in the Scottish mountains with other students from my school. I learned about using camping equipment and dealing with problems like bad weather and getting lost.

I believe I have lots of useful experience to work as an Overland Tour Guide. I would love the opportunity to tell you more about my skills at an interview.

Yours faithfully,
Nicholas Rigby
