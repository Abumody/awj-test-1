Robertson was in art from a young age. But she did not start painting until many years later as she was married had children to take care of. When Anna was 67 years old, her husband died and, as a result, she began to suffer from depression. In order to cope with her loss, she looked for ways to keep herself busy. In her seventies, she most of her time painting. She was completely self-taught. Her painting subjects were mostly taken from the village and farm life around her. Her paintings became famous an art collector saw some of her works hanging in a local shop and bought them all. Her success grew and her paintings were even at the Museum of Modern Arts in New York. She died in 1961 at the age of 101.
