READING 11 (Items 1 - 5)

Read the texts. Are the statements which follow each text True or False?

1.
I first met Ahmed in 1998, just after graduating from university. We were both waiting to be interviewed for the same job, so we started chatting. Actually, I got the job, but that didn't matter, and we soon became close friends.
The writer and Ahmed were good friends at university.
2.
At Indian weddings, the groom goes to the bride's home. When he enters the house, he must take off his shoes. Sometime during the various ceremonies, the bride's sisters 'steal' these shoes. Before the groom is allowed to leave, he has to give these 'thieves' some money to get his shoes back.
The bride's family give the groom a new pair of shoes.
3.
Mount Manaslu in Nepal is the 8th highest mountain in the world. It was first climbed by two Japanese men in 1956. Twenty years later, a team of fifteen South Korean climbers tried to repeat their achievement, but they were all killed. Since then, no one has reached the summit.
Only two climbers have ever climbed Mount Manaslu.
4.
Blood Cancer Relief needs volunteers. Please help us find donors who can provide healthy bone-marrow for our patients. All you have to do is carry out a simple test. The task is simple, but will save many lives. No experience is required. Our doctors will provide all the necessary training.
The charity wants help from doctors and nurses.
5.
By the age of fourteen, Teresa Milanello was already a well-known violinist. Her family were an important part of her success. She played a violin made by her father, who was a skilled craftsman. Later, she taught her younger sister, Maria, to play, and they often performed together.
Teresa's father made musical instruments.
