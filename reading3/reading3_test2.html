Yusra Mardini

Yusra Mardini was born in Damascus, Syria, where she trained as a competitive swimmer from a young age. Her life changed in 2015 when war made daily life unsafe for her family. Training became impossible, and Yusra and her sister decided to leave Syria in search of safety.

Their journey was dangerous. While crossing the Sea from Turkey to Greece in a small boat, the engine stopped, and the boat began to fill with water. Yusra, her sister, and two other passengers jumped into the sea and pushed the boat for several hours until they reached the shore of Lesbos. Without their swimming skills, many lives could have been lost.

After arriving in Germany, Yusra faced new challenges. She had to learn a new language, adapt to a different culture, and restart her swimming training from zero. At first, she struggled with loneliness and exhaustion, but she refused to quit.

In 2016, only one year after fleeing Syria, Yusra competed in the Rio Olympic Games as part of the Refugee Olympic Team. She did not win a medal, but her participation became a symbol of strength and resilience.
